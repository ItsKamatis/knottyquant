<h2>Overview</h2>

<h2>Data and assumptions</h2>

The full private archive contains seven assets, including AAPL, across irregular saved windows from July 2024 through August 2025. The closing accounting panel is narrower: INTC, NDX, NVDA, RUT, SPX, and SPY over eight August 2025 dates.

<DataBoundary>
Licensed quote chains and credentials are not redistributed. Figures and aggregate evidence derive from frozen retained panels, while synthetic workflows remain reproducible. The absence of public quote-level rows limits independent reconstruction from the website alone.
</DataBoundary>

European index carry is estimated expiry by expiry from put–call parity. American equity and ETF carry uses the zero curve and a projected cash-dividend schedule.

$$
C-P=D(T)\left[F(T)-K\right],
\qquad
k=\log\left(\frac{K}{F}\right),
\qquad
w=\sigma^2T.
$$

Contract families are classified before calibration using settlement, root class, and expiry archetype. The classifier is transparent, but it remains an approximation to an authoritative exchange contract master.

</section>

<section id="method">

<span id="surface" class="anchor-alias"></span>

<h2>Method</h2>

Implied volatility is solved as a monotone price root with economic bounds and explicit failure states. The initial volatility bracket is $[10^{-6},3]$, with controlled expansion to 5 and 8, tolerance $10^{-8}$, and an 80-iteration limit.

Each expiry slice is fitted with bounded, deterministic multi-start raw SVI in total variance:

$$
w(k)=a+b\left\{\rho(k-m)+\sqrt{(k-m)^2+\sigma^2}\right\}.
$$

Price-spread uncertainty is mapped into total-variance space using the price-to-variance Jacobian. This aligns the loss more closely with quote-price sensitivity; it does not make raw SVI globally arbitrage free.

<span id="repair" class="anchor-alias"></span>

### Static-arbitrage repair

Fitted slices are evaluated on a regular $(T,k)$ grid. Calendar repair applies least-squares isotonic projection down each maturity column. Strike rows are then adjusted with constrained SLSQP until the discrete butterfly diagnostic satisfies its configured tolerance. Raw and repaired outputs are retained separately.

### Comparison governance

Candidate models use matched repair, paired common contexts, family-date aggregation, disclosed support loss, tail gates, and a de-duplicated full-supported companion universe. A lower pooled error cannot by itself offset a failed family, adverse date, or support collapse.

<section id="implementation">

<h2>Implementation</h2>

The reviewed local source commit exposes the bounded inversion and fitting research path. The inversion refuses invalid bounds and unresolved brackets rather than emitting a numerical value that looks successful.

<CodeExcerpt
  label="src/calculate_iv.py — bracketed inversion at the reviewed source commit"
  code={'def implied_vol_brent(price, F, K, T, D, is_call, *,\n                      sigma_low, sigma_high, tol, max_iter):\n    lb, ub = price_bounds(F, K, D, is_call)\n    if price < lb - 1e-7 or price > ub + 1e-7:\n        return np.nan\n\n    def objective(sigma):\n        return black_price(F, K, T, D, sigma, is_call) - price\n\n    if objective(sigma_low) * objective(sigma_high) > 0:\n        for candidate_high in [5.0, 8.0]:\n            if objective(sigma_low) * objective(candidate_high) <= 0:\n                sigma_high = candidate_high\n                break\n        else:\n            return np.nan\n\n    result = root_scalar(\n        objective, bracket=[sigma_low, sigma_high],\n        method="brentq", xtol=tol, rtol=tol, maxiter=max_iter,\n    )\n    return float(result.root) if result.converged else np.nan'}
/>

European closing prices use analytic Black pricing from the repaired surface. American equity and ETF contracts use a 400-step Cox–Ross–Rubinstein tree with explicit early exercise. Leisen–Reimer and a Crank–Nicolson projected-SOR implementation are retained as independent numerical comparisons rather than described as ground truth.

<section id="results-and-checks">

<span id="weekly" class="anchor-alias"></span>

<h2>Results and checks</h2>

A guarded price-local refinement was allowed to alter at most one eligible nonstandard weekly slice and was accepted only when price error improved without violating variance, parameter, butterfly, calendar-neighbor, or support guards. It changed 7 of 24 August slices.

<ResearchFigure

  width={1086}
  height={405}
  alt="Weekly option refinement results by asset and basket"

The liquidity-basket repaired RMSE moved from 7.4179 to 3.8995; the representative basket moved from 8.6654 to 3.9681; the de-duplicated full chain moved from 6.3036 to 3.1700. A later November control improved by only about 2.6% and 2.0%, so the result supports a narrow family policy rather than a global objective change.

<span id="alternatives" class="anchor-alias"></span>

Several negative or local results remain part of the record. Repair-matched eSSVI survives as a standard-PM-monthly candidate rather than a global replacement. A smoothed local-window repository fit won 80 of 202 exact-support contexts but was materially worse in aggregate. American-aware normalization changed local fits without creating a promoted replacement.

### Exercise style and numerical convergence

The American closing subset contains 1,200 matched American/European tree pairs. Mean model early-exercise premium is 0.004264, the largest observed premium is 0.4172, and no negative premiums are classified. These are model-conditional differences, not trading signals.
