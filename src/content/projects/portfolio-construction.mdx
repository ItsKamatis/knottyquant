<section id="overview">

<h2>Overview</h2>

This project studies the layer between an estimated return distribution and a feasible portfolio. The focus is not a return forecast by itself, but the decisions that follow: covariance shrinkage, downside objectives, benchmark-relative risk, transaction costs, exposure controls, and discrete holding counts.

<div class="result-grid">
  <div><span>Public universe</span><strong>12 synthetic assets</strong></div>
  <div><span>Holding periods</span><strong>72 monthly decisions</strong></div>
  <div><span>Main comparison</span><strong>7 long-only methods</strong></div>
  <div><span>Cardinality</span><strong>Exactly 8 holdings</strong></div>
</div>

The public run is an engineering demonstration. It is designed to expose method behavior and feasibility checks, not to establish alpha or an investable strategy.

</section>

<section id="data-and-assumptions">

<span id="design" class="anchor-alias"></span>

<h2>Data and assumptions</h2>

The published demonstration uses deterministic synthetic asset and factor returns. Twelve assets are rebalanced monthly from January 2020 through December 2025 after a 2018–2019 estimation warm-up. Method development covers 2020–2023; 2024–2025 is already inspected and is labeled accordingly rather than called a pristine test.

<DataBoundary>
The external adapter has been exercised on retained CRSP-derived price history without a live WRDS connection. Those rows, weights, and performance outputs are not distributed. The retained panel is split-adjusted price history without dividends and uses a current-constituent universe, so it carries a survivorship boundary.
</DataBoundary>

Portfolio weights for month $t$ use information dated through $t-1$. Missing returns are not replaced with zero. Before the next rebalance, prior weights drift with realized returns; turnover and costs are computed from those drifted weights.

$$
\hat\mu_i=0.5\bar\mu_i+0.5\bar\mu_{\mathrm{grand}},
\qquad
\tau_t=\frac{1}{2}\sum_i\left|w_{i,t}-\widetilde w_{i,t-1}\right|.
$$

</section>

<section id="method">

<span id="methods" class="anchor-alias"></span>

<h2>Method</h2>

Expected returns use 50% shrinkage toward the cross-sectional grand mean. Covariance uses Ledoit–Wolf shrinkage. The comparison is deliberately curated: each method represents a distinct construction question rather than another solver configuration.

<EvidenceTable label="Portfolio construction methods">
  <table>
    <thead><tr><th>Method</th><th>Objective</th><th>Role</th></tr></thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr><td>Equal weight</td><td>None</td><td>Reference portfolio</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Global minimum variance</td><td>Minimize variance</td><td>Risk-only baseline</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Shrinkage mean–variance</td><td>Return minus risk penalty</td><td>Noisy-forecast allocation</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Maximum estimated Sharpe</td><td>Best solved frontier point</td><td>Explicit GMV fallback when excess return is nonpositive</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Historical joint CVaR</td><td>Minimize empirical 95% loss CVaR</td><td>Downside-risk objective</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Benchmark-relative</td><td>Active utility net of turnover penalty</td><td>Tracking error, turnover, and factor controls</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Exact-cardinality CVaR</td><td>CVaR with binary selection</td><td>Exactly eight holdings</td></tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</EvidenceTable>

The benchmark-relative default uses a 20% position cap, 8% ex-ante tracking-error limit, 30% one-way turnover limit, active Fama–French five-factor plus momentum controls, and a 10 bp one-way transaction-cost model. The cardinality formulation uses 1%–25% weights when selected.

The 130/30 formulation is kept as a mathematical appendix rather than included in the main comparison because the project does not model borrow availability, fees, recalls, financing, margin, or market impact.

</section>

<section id="implementation">

<span id="constraints" class="anchor-alias"></span>

<h2>Implementation</h2>

The continuous problems are expressed in CVXPY. A benchmark-relative weight vector is constrained against the eligible-universe benchmark, prior drifted weights, the covariance estimate, and lagged factor exposures.

<CodeExcerpt
  label="src/constrained_portfolio/optimization.py — benchmark-relative constraints"
  code={'w = cp.Variable(len(assets))\nactive = w - benchmark\nconstraints = [\n    cp.sum(w) == 1,\n    w >= 0,\n    w <= max_weight,\n    cp.quad_form(active, cp.psd_wrap(sigma))\n        <= tracking_error_limit**2,\n]\n\none_way_turnover = 0.5 * (\n    cp.norm1(w - prior) + outside_prior_absolute_weight\n)\nconstraints.append(one_way_turnover <= turnover_limit)\nconstraints.extend([\n    active_factor <= tolerance,\n    active_factor >= -tolerance,\n])'}
/>

Exact-cardinality CVaR is a mixed-integer linear program solved through SciPy’s HiGHS interface. Binary selection variables are linked to continuous weights, and the selected count is fixed before the loss-slack constraints are added.

<CodeExcerpt
  label="src/constrained_portfolio/optimization.py — exact-count and weight-selection links"
  code={'count = np.zeros(n_variables)\ncount[z_slice] = 1.0\nrows.append(count)\nlb.append(float(cardinality))\nub.append(float(cardinality))\n\nfor asset in range(n_assets):\n    upper_link = np.zeros(n_variables)\n    upper_link[asset] = 1.0\n    upper_link[n_assets + asset] = -maximum_weight\n    rows.append(upper_link)\n    lb.append(-np.inf)\n    ub.append(0.0)\n\n    lower_link = np.zeros(n_variables)\n    lower_link[asset] = -1.0\n    lower_link[n_assets + asset] = minimum_weight\n    rows.append(lower_link)\n    lb.append(-np.inf)\n    ub.append(0.0)'}
/>

<span id="validation" class="anchor-alias"></span>

Every solution is checked against its stated budget, bound, tracking-error, turnover, exposure, and integrality conditions. The maximum-Sharpe rule used its documented GMV fallback in 19 of 72 months, and the exact-cardinality method selected eight assets in every published month.

A six-month moving-block bootstrap reports 95% intervals for return, volatility, Sharpe ratio, and maximum drawdown. Every public-demo Sharpe interval crosses zero, so the project makes no statistical-performance claim.

<section id="limitations-and-materials">

<span id="limits" class="anchor-alias"></span>

<h2>Limitations and materials</h2>

<Limitations>
  <div><h3>Public evidence</h3><p>Displayed performance and attribution use synthetic assets and factors. They demonstrate the pipeline, not an empirical strategy.</p></div>
  <div><h3>Retained data</h3><p>The local price panel omits dividends and uses a current-constituent universe, creating survivorship and total-return limitations.</p></div>
  <div><h3>Tail estimation</h3><p>Private CVaR windows contain 18–29 complete monthly scenarios, often leaving only one or two observations in the 95% tail.</p></div>
  <div><h3>Coverage</h3><p>Historical sector classifications and point-in-time benchmark weights are unavailable, so sector constraints and Brinson attribution are omitted.</p></div>
</Limitations>

The [Github repository](https://github.com/ItsKamatis/constrained-portfolio-construction), [Portfolio summary](/assets/data/portfolio/summary.csv), [bootstrap intervals](/assets/data/portfolio/bootstrap_uncertainty.csv), [monthly results](/assets/data/portfolio/monthly_results.csv), and [active-return attribution](/assets/data/portfolio/active_attribution.csv) provide the public demonstration record.
